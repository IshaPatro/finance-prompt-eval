## Calculating T-Bill Discount Basis Yield

Using the formula provided:

$$\text{Yield} = \frac{100 - 94.50}{100} × \frac{360}{182}$$

## Step-by-Step

**Discount:**
$$100 - 94.50 = 5.50$$

**Discount rate (as fraction of face value):**
$$\frac{5.50}{100} = 0.055$$

**Annualization factor (360-day convention):**
$$\frac{360}{182} = 1.978022$$

**Annual yield:**
$$0.055 × 1.978022 = 0.108791 = \boxed{10.88\%}$$

## Note

This is the **bank discount yield** or **discount basis**—the standard convention for quoting U.S. Treasury bills. It's simpler than bond-equivalent yield because T-bills are short-term instruments with no coupons. The 360-day year is a market convention (not a calendar year), and the calculation doesn't involve discounting at a compound rate—it's a simple annualization of the percentage gain.

The investor buys the T-bill at $94.50 and receives $100 at maturity in 182 days, earning a yield of **10.88%** on a discount basis.
