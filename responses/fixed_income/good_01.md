## Setting Up and Solving

Starting with the given equation:
$$500 = \frac{1,000}{(1 + y/2)^{20}}$$

where $y$ is the annualized bond-equivalent yield and we compound semiannually over 20 periods (2 × 10 years).

## Solving for the Semiannual Rate

Rearrange:
$$(1 + y/2)^{20} = \frac{1,000}{500} = 2$$

Take the 20th root of both sides:
$$1 + \frac{y}{2} = 2^{1/20}$$

$$1 + \frac{y}{2} = 1.035589$$

$$\frac{y}{2} = 0.035589$$

## Annualize the Rate

Multiply by 2 to get the annual bond-equivalent yield:
$$y = 2 × 0.035589 = 0.071178 = \boxed{7.12\%}$$

## Verification

Check: $500 × (1.035589)^{20} = 500 × 2.0 = $1,000 ✓

The **yield to maturity (bond-equivalent, semiannual convention) is 7.12%**.

Note: This is slightly lower than the 7.18% I calculated earlier using annual compounding—the difference reflects the effect of the semiannual compounding convention used in U.S. bond markets.
