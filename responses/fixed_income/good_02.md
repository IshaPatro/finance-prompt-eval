## Calculating Bond-Equivalent Yield (Investment Yield)

Using the formula provided:

$$\text{BEY} = \frac{100 - 94.50}{94.50} × \frac{365}{182}$$

## Step-by-Step

**Gain:**
$$100 - 94.50 = 5.50$$

**Return on actual investment:**
$$\frac{5.50}{94.50} = 0.058201$$

**Annualization factor (365-day year):**
$$\frac{365}{182} = 2.005495$$

**Bond-equivalent yield:**
$$0.058201 × 2.005495 = 0.116820 = \boxed{11.68\%}$$

## Comparison to Discount Basis

- **Bank discount yield** (previous problem): 10.88%
- **Bond-equivalent yield**: 11.68%

The bond-equivalent yield is higher because it:
1. **Divides by the price paid** ($94.50), not face value—showing the true return on the investor's actual capital
2. **Uses a 365-day year**, matching calendar reality rather than the market's 360-day convention

The investor actually earns a **bond-equivalent yield of 11.68%**.
