## Step 1: STRIP A's Implied 1-Year Rate

$$r_1 = \frac{10,000}{9,708.74} - 1 = 1.030000 - 1 = 0.030000 = \boxed{3.0\%}$$

## Step 2: STRIP B's Implied 2-Year Compounded Rate

$$r_2 = \left(\frac{10,000}{8,734.39}\right)^{1/2} - 1$$

$$= (1.144859)^{1/2} - 1$$

$$= 1.069886 - 1 = 0.069886 = \boxed{6.9886\%}$$

## Step 3: Solve for the 1-Year Forward Rate

Using the no-arbitrage condition:

$$(1 + r_2)^2 = (1 + r_1) × (1 + f)$$

$$(1.069886)^2 = (1.030000) × (1 + f)$$

$$1.144816 = 1.030000 × (1 + f)$$

$$1 + f = \frac{1.144816}{1.030000} = 1.112057$$

$$f = 0.112057 = \boxed{11.21\%}$$

The 1-year forward rate for year 2 is **11.21%**.

This makes sense: STRIP A yields only 3% in year 1, so to lock in the 6.99% average return available through STRIP B, year 2 must offer a much higher rate (11.21%) to balance the low first-year return.
