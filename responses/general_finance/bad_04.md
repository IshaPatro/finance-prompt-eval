Current yield = annual coupon ÷ price = $50.50 / $896.10 = **5.64%**.

That's the number you asked for, but I'd flag that it understates the bond's actual return here, and by more than a rounding-error margin. The current yield captures only the coupon stream against the price — it ignores that you're buying at a $103.90 discount to face and will collect the full $1,000 at maturity. That pull-to-par is real return, and over 8 years it's substantial.

The yield to maturity, which folds in both the coupons and that capital gain (and the time value of getting par back in the future), is **6.75%**. That's the figure that answers "what will I actually earn holding this to maturity," and for a discount bond it always sits above the current yield, which in turn sits above the 5.05% coupon rate — the three fan out in that order precisely because the bond trades below par.

So if you genuinely want current yield, it's 5.64%. If you want *the* yield in the sense most fixed-income contexts mean it, 6.75% is the one.
