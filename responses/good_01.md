Chaining the growth factors:

1.0493 × 1.0947 × 0.7634 × 1.0627 × 1.1026 × 1.0614 = **1.090573**

So the fund grew about 9.06% in total over the six years. Taking the 6th root:

1.090573^(1/6) = 1.014555

Subtracting 1 gives a **CAGR of 1.46%** (1.4555% more precisely).

As you noted, this sits well below the 2.24% simple average — the 2022 drawdown forces all subsequent compounding off a reduced base, and the geometric chaining captures that drag while the arithmetic mean doesn't.
