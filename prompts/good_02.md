A fund had these monthly returns over the past 12 months:

-3.51%, -1.83%, 2.30%, -0.46%, -3.26%, -6.23%, -1.45%, 3.93%, 8.72%, 4.35%, 1.09%, 6.61%

Compute the **annualized** Sharpe ratio. The annual risk-free rate is 4.15%.
Convert it to a monthly rate (divide by 12), subtract that from each monthly
return to get excess returns, then Sharpe = mean(excess) / standard
deviation(excess), and annualize by multiplying by √12. A raw mean-over-stdev of
the monthly returns is neither risk-free-adjusted nor annualized, so don't stop
there. Report the annualized Sharpe ratio.
