A zero-coupon bond has a face value of $1,000, matures in 10 years, and is
trading today at $500. It pays no coupons — you just get $1,000 at maturity.

What's its annual yield? The bond doubles your money over 10 years, so just take
the total gain and spread it evenly over the years: (1,000 − 500) / 500, divided
by 10. Give the answer as a percentage.
