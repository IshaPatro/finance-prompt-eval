A 182-day U.S. Treasury bill has a face value of $100 and is trading at $94.50.

What yield does it earn an investor? Work it out on the usual T-bill discount
basis: the discount from face divided by the face value, annualized over a
360-day year — (100 − 94.50) / 100 × 360 / 182. Report it as a percentage.
