A zero-coupon bond has a face value of $1,000, matures in 10 years, and is
trading today at $500. It pays no coupons — the only cash flow is $1,000 at
maturity.

Find its yield to maturity on the U.S. bond-equivalent (semiannual) convention.
The price is the face value discounted over the whole term:

    500 = 1,000 / (1 + y/2)^(2 × 10)

Solve for the semiannual rate and annualize it by multiplying by 2. Don't spread
the total gain evenly across the years — that's simple interest and ignores
compounding, which overstates the yield. Report the YTM as a percentage.
