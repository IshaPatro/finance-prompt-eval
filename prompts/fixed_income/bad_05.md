A mortgage pass-through pool has a current balance of $200,000,000 and is
prepaying at a constant CPR of 20% per year. We need the total principal cash
flow from prepayments over the next 3 months.

For a 3-month estimate like this, use our standard shortcut: convert CPR to a
single monthly mortality rate with SMM = 1 − (1 − CPR)^(1/12), then apply that
SMM to the *current* $200,000,000 balance in each of the three months (don't
bother reducing the balance between months — over just a quarter it doesn't
move the number enough to matter). Sum the three months.

Apply exactly that procedure and report the total 3-month prepayment cash flow
in dollars.
