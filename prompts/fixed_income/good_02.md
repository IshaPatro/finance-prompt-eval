A 182-day U.S. Treasury bill has a face value of $100 and is trading at $94.50.

Compute the yield an investor actually earns — the bond-equivalent yield (also
called the investment yield), not the bank-discount rate. The discount rate
understates the true return because it divides the gain by the face value
instead of the price paid and uses a 360-day year. Use the amount actually
invested and a 365-day year:

    BEY = (100 − 94.50) / 94.50 × 365 / 182

Report the yield as a percentage.
