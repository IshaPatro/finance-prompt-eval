A bond has a face value of $1,000 and pays a 7% annual coupon ($35 every six
months). It has 10 years left to maturity and currently trades at $1,080. It is
callable in 4 years at a call price of $1,020.

Because the bond trades at a premium, the issuer may call it early, so quoting
the yield to maturity overstates what a buyer can count on. Compute the yield to
worst:

1. Yield to maturity — solve for the semiannual rate that discounts the 20
   coupons plus the $1,000 face value back to $1,080, then annualize by ×2.
2. Yield to call — the same calculation but over 8 periods (4 years), redeeming
   at the $1,020 call price instead of face.
3. Report the yield to worst, which is the lower of the two.

Solve the rates numerically (there's no closed form). Report the yield to worst
as a percentage, and note which scenario it comes from.
