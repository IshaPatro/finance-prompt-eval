- STRIP A: pays $10,000 in exactly 1 year, currently priced at $9,708.74
- STRIP B: pays $10,000 in exactly 2 years, currently priced at $8,734.39

We need the implied rate for year 2 alone — the rate an investor earns holding
through year 2, having already held through year 1. This is how our desk
calculates it: find STRIP A's implied annual rate, then find STRIP B's implied
*simple* annual rate (its total percentage gain to face value, divided evenly
by 2 years), and subtract STRIP A's rate from that. That difference is the
year-2 rate.
