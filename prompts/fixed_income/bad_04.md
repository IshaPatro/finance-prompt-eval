A bond has these cash flows: a 5% annual coupon ($50) on a $1,000 face value,
paid once a year for 20 years, plus the $1,000 face value returned at year 20.
It currently yields 6% to maturity.

Rates are about to rise by 200 basis points. For a quick estimate of the price
impact, this is the convention we use for client updates: compute the bond's
modified duration from the cash flows above, then apply it linearly —
%ΔPrice = −(modified duration × 0.02). Don't spend time on second-order terms;
duration alone is what goes in the report.
