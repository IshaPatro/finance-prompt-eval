A bond has a face value of $1,000 and pays a 7% annual coupon ($35 every six
months). It has 10 years left to maturity and currently trades at $1,080. It is
callable in 4 years at $1,020.

What yield does a buyer earn at today's price? Just compute the yield to maturity
— discount the coupons and the $1,000 face value over the full 10 years back to
the $1,080 price. Report the yield as a percentage.
