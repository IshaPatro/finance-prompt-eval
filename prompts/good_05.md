A loan has a nominal APR of 16.50% compounded monthly (12 periods per year).

Compute the effective annual rate (EAR) — the rate that actually accrues over a
year once the within-year compounding is taken into account:

    EAR = (1 + APR / 12) ** 12 − 1

Because interest compounds monthly, the EAR is higher than the quoted APR;
quoting the APR back is wrong. Report the EAR as a percentage.
